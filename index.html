Summary

The analysis reveals that gender was the most significant factor in determining survival on the Titanic, with women having a survival rate more than 4 times higher than men. This aligns with the "women and children first" evacuation protocol.

Conclusion

Data-Driven Insights

Survival Factors

Gender emerged as the primary determinant of survival, overshadowing economic class and age factors.

Social Protocols

The "women and children first" maritime tradition was clearly evident in the survival patterns.

Data Quality

Despite missing data in age and cabin information, clear patterns emerged from available demographics.

Analytical Recommendations
